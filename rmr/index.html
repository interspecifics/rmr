In this project, we address hyper-personalized consumption practices driven by algorithm suggestion logics used in music streaming platforms, which follow commercially-oriented formulas based on genre clustering inducing manufactured demand. We propose the development of a generative neural audio system that exposes the homogenizing effects recurrent algorithmic strategies have on the aesthetics of music. The system will be daily trained with samples from the most consumed genres and styles on digital platforms, extracting and destructuring its main features to continuously and uninterruptedly generate new sound derive from its analysis data. A non-stop music production machine as a living metaphor of a cultural process intervened by a capitalist market, in which over-consumption equals over-training. The outcome is a sound art piece in itself produced by a meta creative agent in which the previous sample materiality dissolves to such an extent that resembles noise over time. The piece will exist on a web server from which users can browse and share the list of generated audios, or listen in a continuous radio format. And in the gallery as a local listening online station along with visualizations from music charts and the AI computing activity.
